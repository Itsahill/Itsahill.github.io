What Is Gaming?

How do you define the activity of playing video games? Simply put, it's using some electronic device to interact with something and have fun. But to describe the medium like this is to do it a disservice. Gaming is a complex, multifaceted activity that can be a soft and gentle experience or a blood-pumping adrenaline nightmare, and everything in between. They can calm you, confuse you, raise your heart rate, crush you, and raise you higher. Games can be a simple pleasure or an art form, and to discount them as simple entertainment would be the same as saying that music just sounds good or movies are nice to look at.

Okay, enough pretentious pretext; I want to tell you what gaming is like from MY perspective, through MY experiences, and why I do it. To me, playing games is something I do to because I enjoy challenge and the feeling of achievement I get when I earn a hard-fought victory(when it's something I want to do, not like work). Challenge is probably a main point of why I enjoy gaming, and though I can complain about some part or other of a difficult game, it's (usually) from a place of love.

Who Are Games For?

This question is kind of redundant considering the previous section, but games are for anyone and everyone. Everyone can enjoy games, but not every game is enjoyed by everyone; it depends on what kind of experience you want. Looking to unwind with a relaxing, simple game? Maybe try Animal Crossing or something similar. Want a game that's easy to pick up and put down? Tetris is right there? If you're looking for some action, there's a wide array of combat games, from beat-em-ups to fighters to shooters. The point is that there's no way someone out there will hate every video game ever. If you can find the right one, they'll fold.

My preferred games are generally something with a lot of action and challenge to them. I regularly play games where I fail often, and get back up to do it again. Some examples of this would be Hyper Light Drifter(pictured here), Dark Souls, and ULTRAKILL. These games require a lot of practice and focus from me, and really get my heart beating in the tense moments. Doing well in these games makes me feel a sense of accomplishment I don't get anywhere else.

When Can You Game?

Gaming isn't usually a hobby you have to plan around for, unless you're playing with friends for some predetermined event. The simple answer is: whenever you want. But what game you play in that time is usually up to what you're feeling at that time. If you need to get away from the stress of work, maybe try something relaxing. If you're bored out of your mind, think of picking up something engaging. If you feel like solving puzzles, try something confusing. But if you are feeling any of these emotions, try to hold it until your attention isn't required by something or someone. Or play Tetris in class, it's your decision(I do not condone this).

Appropriate Gaming Times:

  • At home when not busy
  • On long trips
  • When you need to kill time
  • Probably not during funerals (personal experience)

Where Can You Game?

If you're not at home, maybe you have a handheld console; a 3DS or Switch or something of the like. Honestly, if you felt like it, you could even bring a console and a monitor to a place with enough electrical sockets. Who's going to stop you? The police? They don't care. On-site staff? How Do You Game?

How you should or are supposed to game is a great question, and not really one I can answer. Every game has its differences, no matter how minute they are, so the actual way you're supposed to play is determined by the game's directions and your personal preferences. Maybe you're an old man who thinks the only way to play Halo is on an Xbox controller. That's fine. Maybe you're a freak of nature that thinks fighting games should be played on a keyboard and mouse. That's okay, I guess. Maybe. Not really. But the point is, I cannot answer this question for you directly. I can only give you examples of how people do this already.

I want to make this specification because not every preference will fit every game. Playing a fighting game with a keyboard is strange and awful and I don't like you, but it's possible to do it and do it well. Trying to play a fighting game on a Guitar Hero controller, while theoretically possible, is not going to be as convenient. Same with playing Call of Duty on a dance pad, or literally anything other than Just Dance with a Kinect.

Why Should You Game?

The simple reason is: because it's fun! You don't play video games because you have to, or you'll die if you don't. But a fuller explanation would be because it's an escape, in some shape or form. Obviously, you wouldn't actually fight monsters if you were bored, or do farmwork if you wanted to unwind, but in an abstracted perspective (from a screen), these things let you act out a more exciting or relaxing life. They let you have experiences, either alone or with friends, that you couldn't have anywhere else. Movies don't have that sense of interactivity, physical hobbies don't have that sense of vicariousness that lets you experience something extraordinary.

Gaming lets me hang out with my friends even when we aren't physically close, and have great experiences alongside that. I've made great memories with friends and family, and some games have left a lasting impact on me as a person. I've been playing video games for about as long as I can remember, and I can't say I've regretted my decision. Gaming is an activity for everyone. If you didn't think that before, I hope you do now.
